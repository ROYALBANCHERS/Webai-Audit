Complete Guide to Competitor Analysis

Discover your competitors' strategies and find opportunities to outrank them

Competitor analysis is the systematic process of identifying, evaluating, and understanding your competitors' strategies, strengths, and weaknesses. Identifying Your Competitors

The first step is knowing who you're competing against. Competitors generally fall into three categories:

Direct Competitors

Businesses offering the same products/services to the same target audience. These are your primary competition.

Example: For an e-commerce shoe store, direct competitors are other online shoe retailers like Zappos, Shoes.com, and DSW.

Indirect Competitors

Businesses offering different products that solve the same problem or satisfy the same need.

Example: For a shoe store, indirect competitors include department stores, clothing retailers with shoe sections, and subscription shoe services.

SERP Competitors

Websites ranking for your target keywords, regardless of their business model. These are your SEO competitors.

Example: Bloggers, review sites, and comparison engines that rank for "best running shoes" are SERP competitors even if they don't sell shoes.

Backlink Profile Analysis

Quality backlinks are crucial for SEO. Analyze competitors' link profiles to find opportunities:

Link Quality Indicators

  • Links from relevant, authoritative sites in your niche
  • Natural anchor text distribution (not over-optimized)
  • Diverse link types (editorial, profiles, directories)
  • Links from content (not just footers/sidebars)

Link Building Opportunities

  • Reclaim unlinked mentions of your brand
  • Replicate broken competitor backlinks
  • Guest post on sites that link to competitors
  • Create better resources for linked content

Step-by-Step Analysis Framework

Follow this structured approach for effective competitor analysis:

1

Identify 5-10 Primary Competitors

Use search results, industry knowledge, and tools to create your competitor list. Include both direct and SERP competitors.

2

Gather Baseline Data

Collect key metrics for each competitor: traffic, keywords, backlinks, domain authority, and social followers.

3

Perform Deep-Dive Analysis

Analyze top-performing content, backlink profiles, technical SEO, and content strategy for each competitor.

4

Identify Gaps & Opportunities

Compare findings against your own performance to find gaps and opportunities you can exploit.

5

Create Action Plan

Prioritize opportunities based on impact and effort. Create specific tasks and timelines.

6

Monitor & Iterate

Regularly re-analyze competitors to track changes and adjust your strategy. Set up quarterly reviews.
