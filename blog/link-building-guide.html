Link Building Strategies Complete Guide

Build quality backlinks that actually boost your rankings

50 min read Updated Jan 15, 2025

Let's be real for a second: link building is probably the most challenging part of SEO. It's also one of the most important. Google still treats backlinks as one of their top ranking factors—they're basically votes of confidence from other websites. But here's the thing: not all links are created equal, and the old spammy tactics don't work anymore (and can actually hurt you). This guide covers white-hat link building strategies that actually work in 2024.

Why Backlinks Still Matter So Much

Google's PageRank: Still Relevant After All These Years

When Larry Page and Sergey Brin founded Google, they revolutionized search by treating links as votes of confidence. The core idea hasn't changed much:

  • Every link pointing to your page is like a vote
  • Links from authoritative sites carry way more weight
  • Relevant links from your niche are worth more
  • Link equity flows through your site via internal links

Better Rankings

Backlinks are still in Google's top 3 ranking factors—they matter that much

Faster Discovery

Links help Google find and index your new pages more quickly

Referral Traffic

Quality links send actual visitors who might become customers

Understanding Link Quality

Here's the thing about link building: quality absolutely destroys quantity. One link from a respected site in your niche is worth more than hundreds from random, low-quality sites.

Domain Authority (DA)

DA is a score (1-100) that predicts how well a website will rank. Higher DA sites can pass more link equity to you.

Rule of thumb: Aim for DA 40+ when possible, but don't obsess over it—a relevant DA 30 link beats a random DA 70 link any day

Relevance is Key

Google's gotten a lot smarter about this. Links from sites in your niche or related industries carry way more weight than random ones.

Best practice: A DA 30 link from a relevant site in your industry is worth more than a DA 70 link from a completely unrelated site

Link Placement Matters

Where the link appears on the page actually affects how much value it passes:

  • Best: Right in the main content body
  • Okay: In sidebar widgets or author bios
  • Worst: Footer links or sitewide sidebar links

Dofollow vs Nofollow

The rel attribute tells Google how to treat each link. Most links you'll get should be dofollow:

<a href="...">

Dofollow (default): Passes link equity—the good stuff

<a href="..." rel="nofollow">

Nofollow: Doesn't pass equity (but can still send traffic, which is nice)

Guest Blogging: Still Works When Done Right

Guest posting took a hit after Google's crackdown on spammy guest posts, but it's still one of the most effective white-hat strategies—when you do it the right way.

My Guest Posting Process

  1. 1
    Find Quality Target Sites

    Use Google search operators like "your keyword" + "write for us" or "guest post" to find opportunities in your niche

  2. 2
    Vet the Sites Carefully

    Check their DA, whether they have real traffic, and if their content is actually good. Avoid link farms at all costs

  3. 3
    Do Your Homework

    Read 5-10 of their posts to understand their tone, style, and what topics resonate with their audience

  4. 4
    Pitch Something Unique

    Don't propose topics they've covered a dozen times. Bring fresh angles or original research they haven't seen before

  5. 5
    Deliver Exceptional Content

    Exceed their expectations on length and quality. Make something so good they'd be crazy not to publish it

Guest Posting Done Right

  • Only write for sites with real, engaged audiences
  • Keep it to 1-2 contextual links—don't be spammy about it
  • Link to your best relevant content, not just your homepage
  • Actually engage with comments after your post goes live
  • Promote your guest post on social media—everyone wins

Broken Link Building: The Win-Win Strategy

Here's a strategy that's actually helpful: find broken links on relevant sites and offer your content as a replacement. You help them fix a broken link, and you get a backlink. Everyone wins.

How Broken Link Building Works

Step 1: Find resource pages in your niche

Search for things like "your keyword" + "resources" or "useful links"

Step 2: Use tools to find broken links

Check My Links extension, Ahrefs, or Screaming Frog are your friends here

Step 3: Create or find replacement content

Make sure you have something that covers the same topic even better than the broken page did

Step 4: Reach out to the site owner

Be helpful and polite—point out the broken link and suggest your content as an alternative

The Skyscraper Technique

This technique, popularized by Brian Dean from Backlinko, is all about finding content that's already working well and making it even better. Simple concept, powerful results.

The 3-Step Skyscraper Process

1
Find Content That's Already Killing It

Use Ahrefs or SEMrush to find content with lots of backlinks in your niche

2
Create Something Even Better

Go deeper—more examples, better design, updated data, original research

3
Reach Out to People Who Linked

Contact everyone who linked to the original and let them know about your improved version

Ways to Make Content Undeniably Better

  • Go 50% deeper than the original—cover topics they missed
  • Update outdated information with fresh data and statistics
  • Add original research, surveys, or case studies nobody else has
  • Better visuals—charts, infographics, videos that actually help
  • Improve the design and make it more readable
  • Add practical examples and templates people can use

Digital PR Strategies That Work

Digital PR is about earning links through newsworthy content and building relationships with journalists. It's more challenging but the links you get are incredibly valuable.

Newsjacking

Piggyback on trending news stories with your expert take or data. It's timely and journalists love it.

Example: When AI is all over the news, publish "How AI is Actually Transforming [Your Industry]"

Original Studies & Surveys

Publish your own data and research. Journalists and bloggers love citing original studies.

Example: "We surveyed 500 marketers about their 2024 SEO budgets—here's what we learned"

Free Tools & Calculators

Create genuinely useful tools that people naturally want to link to and share.

Example: ROI calculator, cost comparison tool, savings estimator—make it useful

Awards & Lists

Create annual awards or curated lists. People who make the cut will almost always link back to you.

Example: "Top 100 [Industry] Blogs You Should Be Reading in 2024"

Journalist Outreach Platforms

These platforms connect journalists with expert sources—perfect for building relationships and earning links:

  • HARO (Help a Reporter Out) - Free queries from journalists 3x/day. Seriously, sign up.
  • Qwoted - Connect with journalists who need expert quotes in your niche
  • ResponseSource - UK-based journalist requests if that's your market
  • SourceBottle - Another great platform for journalist connections

Resource Page Link Building

Lots of websites maintain resource pages with curated links to useful content. Getting listed is one of the most sustainable link building tactics out there.

Finding Resource Pages

Use these Google search operators to find resource pages in your niche:

"your keyword" + "resources" + inurl:resources.html
"your keyword" + "useful links" + inurl:links
"your keyword" + "recommended sites" + "add url"

Pro Tips for Resource Pages

  • Check if the page has been updated recently (look at the footer)
  • Look for broken links—those are your golden opportunities
  • Verify the page actually gets traffic (tools like SimilarWeb can help)
  • Personalize your outreach by mentioning specific resources you liked on their page

Email Outreach Templates That Actually Get Responses

Your outreach can make or break your link building efforts. The key? Being personal, concise, and providing value upfront.

Guest Post Pitch Email

  • [Point 1]
  • [Point 2]
  • [Point 3]

[Your Name]

Anchor Text: Don't Mess This Up

Anchor text is the clickable text in a hyperlink. It helps search engines understand what the linked page is about—but you've got to be careful not to overdo it.

Exact Match

"blue running shoes"

Powerful but risky if overused

Partial Match

"best shoes for running"

Good balance of relevance and safety

Branded

"Nike official site"

Natural and safe

Generic

"click here" / "learn more"

Natural but not very descriptive

Anchor Text Best Practices

  • Keep it natural and varied—don't use the same anchor text everywhere
  • Make sure the anchor text matches the page you're linking to
  • For guest posts, stick to branded or URL anchors—play it safe
  • Whatever you do, don't over-optimize—too many exact-match anchors looks spammy

Link Reclamation: Getting Back What You Lost

Sometimes you lose backlinks you used to have. Link reclamation is about recovering that lost value by fixing the issues that caused you to lose the links in the first place.

404 Error Links

Find sites linking to pages on your site that return 404 errors, then reach out and ask them to update to the correct URL.

Tool: Google Search Console > Coverage > Excluded > 404 errors

Redirect Issues

Check if your redirects are working properly and not losing link equity along the way.

Tool: Ahrefs > Backlinks with "Lost" filter

Unlinked Brand Mentions

Find places where people mention your brand but don't link to you, and politely ask for a link.

Tool: Ahrefs Content Explorer > Mentions tab

Broken Backlinks

When a site that linked to you goes down or changes, find the new URL or ask them to remove the broken link.

Action: Monitor your backlink profile regularly

Unlinked Brand Mentions: Easy Wins

People are talking about you or your business online—but they're not always linking to you. These unlinked mentions are low-hanging fruit for building links.

How to Find Unlinked Mentions

  • Google Alerts: Set up alerts for your brand name, products, and key people—it's free and effective
  • Ahrefs Mentions: Content Explorer has a "Mentions" tab that's gold for this
  • BuzzSumo: Great for monitoring when your brand gets mentioned across the web
  • Mention.net: Real-time mention tracking with alerts

Outreach Template for Unlinked Mentions

Subject: Quick question about [article title]

Hi [Name],


Thanks for mentioning [Your Brand] in your article on [topic]—really appreciate the shout-out!


I noticed the mention didn't include a link to our site. Would you mind adding one? Here's our URL: [your URL]


No worries if not—just thought I'd ask. Thanks again!


Best,
[Your Name]

Link Building Mistakes That Can Get You in Trouble

Buying Links

This one's straightforward: it violates Google's guidelines and can result in manual penalties. Always earn links through providing value, not payment.

PBNs (Private Blog Networks)

Networks of sites created solely for link building. Google has gotten scarily good at detecting these—honestly, it's not worth the risk.

Excessive Link Exchanges

"I'll link to you if you link to me" schemes can be seen as manipulative. Natural reciprocal links happen, but don't overdo it.

Article Directories

Low-quality article submission sites have been devalued by Google for years. They're not worth your time.

Comment Spam

Dropping links in blog comments just for backlinks. Most are nofollow anyway, and it's just not a good look.

Forum Spam

Dropping links in forums without adding any real value. Most forums use nofollow, and it's obvious what you're doing.

Ignoring Relevance

Getting links from totally unrelated sites doesn't help much. Context matters more than ever.

Over-Optimized Anchor Text

Too many exact-match keywords in your anchor text looks unnatural and can trigger penalties. Mix it up and keep it real.
