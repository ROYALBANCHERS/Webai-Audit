So What Exactly Is Local SEO?

The Simple Definition

Local SEO is all about making sure your business shows up when people nearby are searching for what you offer. It's not just about ranking - it's about being visible at the exact moment someone in your area needs you. Pretty powerful, right?

The Local Pack

You know those top 3 business listings with map pins that show up above regular results? That's the Local Pack - prime real estate!

Localized Organic Results

These are regular search results, but Google gives them a local twist based on where the searcher is and what's nearby.

Types of Local Searches

"Near Me" Searches

"restaurants near me"

Location-Based

"Open Now" Searches

Mastering Your Google Business Profile

Remember Google My Business? It's called Google Business Profile now, and it's absolutely essential for local SEO. If you only do one thing from this guide, make it this one.

Your Complete GBP Setup Checklist

Claim or Verify Your Business

Go through Google's verification - they'll send a postcard, do a video call, or sometimes verify instantly

Pick Your Primary Category

Choose the most specific category that fits what you do - this is huge for rankings

Add Secondary Categories

You can add up to 9 more categories to help Google understand what you offer

Get Your Business Name Right

Use your exact real-world name - no keyword stuffing or you'll get flagged

NAP Information (Critical!)

Your Name, Address, and Phone must match your website and every other listing exactly

Website URL

Link to a location-specific page if you have multiple locations

Service Areas

If you serve customers at their location (like plumbers or cleaners), define your service area

Business Hours

Set accurate hours and don't forget to update for holidays - nothing frustrates customers more

Write a Compelling Description

You've got 750 characters - tell your story and naturally weave in your keywords

Photos & Videos Matter

Add your logo, cover photo, interior shots, exterior shots, team photos, and examples of your work

Want to Really Stand Out? Try These Tips

  • Post regularly - share updates, special offers, and events at least once a week
  • Respond to ALL reviews - yes, even the negative ones (especially the negative ones!)
  • Get ahead of questions by adding Q&A content yourself
  • Turn on messaging so customers can reach you directly
  • Show off what you sell with the products feature
  • Keep everything updated - especially your hours during holidays

NAP Consistency: The Secret Sauce of Local SEO

Okay, here's something that trips up a lot of businesses: your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) needs to be EXACTLY the same everywhere online. Seriously, one wrong digit or missing "Suite" can hurt you. Let's get this sorted.

What Are Citations?

A citation is any online mention of your business's Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP). Citations can appear on:

  • Business directories (Yelp, Yellow Pages, etc.)
  • Review sites (TripAdvisor, Angie's List)
  • Local business associations
  • Industry-specific directories
  • Social media profiles

NAP Consistency Best Practices

  • Use exact same format everywhere
  • Include suite/unit numbers consistently
  • Use tracking phone numbers carefully
  • Match your GBP exactly
  • Avoid multiple phone numbers
  • Don't use "800" numbers as primary
  • Monitor for incorrect citations
  • Audit citations quarterly

Getting and Managing Reviews (Yes, They're That Important)

Reviews are basically your online reputation - and Google pays attention. They affect your rankings and they're often the first thing potential customers look at. Let's make sure you're handling them right.

Getting More Reviews

  • Ask satisfied customers directly
  • Email customers after purchase
  • Use QR codes at your location
  • Include review links in email signature
  • Offer incentives (carefully - check platform rules)
  • Make it easy with direct links

Responding to Reviews

  • Respond to ALL reviews (positive and negative)
  • Be professional and courteous
  • Thank customers for positive reviews
  • Address issues in negative reviews
  • Take conversations offline when needed
  • Show you care about feedback

Review Response Templates

Positive Review Response

"Thank you so much for the 5-star review, [Name]! We're thrilled to hear you had a great experience with us. We look forward to serving you again soon!"

Negative Review Response

"We're sorry to hear about your experience, [Name]. We'd love to make it right. Please contact us at [phone/email] so we can address this directly."

On-Page Local SEO

Optimize your website pages to signal local relevance to search engines.

Key On-Page Elements

Title Tags

Include location: "Best Coffee Shop in Downtown Portland"

Meta Descriptions

Mention location and include local keywords naturally

NAP in Footer or Contact

Schema markup for your business address and phone

Location Landing Pages

Create pages for each service area or location

Local Schema Markup

Implement LocalBusiness schema for rich results

LocalBusiness Schema Example

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Your Business Name",
  "image": "https://example.com/photo.jpg",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main St",
    "addressLocality": "City",
    "addressRegion": "State",
    "postalCode": "12345"
  },
  "telephone": "+1-555-123-4567",
  "priceRange": "$$"
}
</script>
