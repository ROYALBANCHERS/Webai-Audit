1. What is Local SEO and Why Should You Care?

Local SEO is all about showing up when people in your area search for what you offer. Think about it - when you're looking for "pizza near me" or "plumber in [your city]," you want results close by, right? That's what local SEO does for your business.

Why Local SEO is Worth Your Time

  • Nearly half of all Google searches are looking for something local
  • 72% of people who search locally visit a store within 5 miles
  • 78% of local mobile searches lead to someone buying offline
  • 88% of local searchers call or visit within a day

What Local Search Results Look Like

  • Map Pack: Those top 3 businesses with pins on the map
  • Local Business Listings: The info cards with your business details
  • Localized Organic Results: Regular rankings that favor local businesses
  • Google Maps: Full business info, directions, reviews, the works

2. Making Your Google Business Profile Shine

Your Google Business Profile (you might know it as Google My Business) is basically your online storefront. It's the single most important thing for local SEO, so let's get it right.

Setting Things Up the Right Way

  1. Claim your spot: Head to google.com/business and claim or verify your listing
  2. Pick your category wisely: This matters more than you think - choose the most relevant one
  3. Use your real business name: No keywords, no location stuffing - just your actual name
  4. Verify you exist: Google will send a postcard or ask for video verification
  5. Fill out everything: Hours, phone number, website - the whole nine yards

The Essentials That Matter

Your Business Name

  • Use exactly what's on your signage - no funny business
  • Don't add keywords or your city to the name (Google hates that)
  • Keep it consistent everywhere you're listed online

Business Categories

  • Your primary category should be as specific as possible
  • You can add up to 9 total categories if they're relevant
  • Only pick categories for services you actually offer

Your Hours

  • Set accurate hours and keep them updated
  • Add special hours for holidays and events
  • Nothing frustrates customers like showing up when you're closed

Your Description

  • You get 750 characters - use them wisely
  • Tell people what makes you different
  • Naturally include relevant keywords (don't stuff them in)
  • Focus on what your customers actually care about

Attributes

  • Select everything that applies (wheelchair accessible, women-led, etc.)
  • Keep these updated as your business evolves

Google Posts - Free Advertising, Basically

These show up right in your Business Profile and keep things fresh:

  • What's New: Share updates and news about your business
  • Offers: Promotions, coupons, sales - bring people in the door
  • Products: Show off specific products you offer
  • Events: Let people know what's coming up

Making the Most of Google Posts

  • Post at least once a week - consistency counts
  • Use good images (750x750 pixels is the sweet spot)
  • Include clear call-to-action buttons
  • Write headlines that make people want to click
  • Most posts expire after a week (except events and offers)

The Q&A Section

  • Answer the questions customers ask most often
  • Think of it as extra opportunities to rank
  • Keep answers helpful but concise
  • Add real questions you get from customers

3. Getting Your Business Listed Everywhere

Citations are just mentions of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP for short) on other websites. The key is keeping them consistent across the web - Google gets confused when your info doesn't match.

NAP Consistency is Non-Negotiable

  • Name: Exactly as it appears on your sign
  • Address: Complete and formatted the same way everywhere
  • Phone: Your main local number
  • Website: Same URL everywhere (www or no www - pick one)

Where to Get Listed

The Big Data Aggregators

These four feed data to hundreds of other sites - get listed here and it spreads everywhere:

  • Neustar Localeze
  • UberMedia (formerly Axciom/Factual)
  • Infogroup
  • Foursquare

Must-Have Directories

  • Google Business Profile (obviously)
  • Bing Places for Business
  • Yelp
  • Apple Maps (via Maps Connect)
  • Facebook
  • Yellow Pages
  • Better Business Bureau
  • Your local Chamber of Commerce

Industry-Specific Sites

  • Restaurants: TripAdvisor, OpenTable, Zomato
  • Home Services: Angi, Thumbtack, Porch
  • Medical: Healthgrades, Vitals, WebMD
  • Legal: Avvo, Justia, Lawyers.com
  • Automotive: Cars.com, Edmunds, RepairPal

4. Getting and Managing Reviews Like a Pro

Reviews are huge for local SEO. The quantity and quality both matter - Google's paying attention, and so are your potential customers.

Why Reviews Are So Important

  • They help you rank: Reviews are a direct ranking factor
  • They get you clicks: Those star ratings stand out in search results
  • They build trust: People trust what other people say
  • They give you feedback: Learn what you're doing right (and wrong)

How to Get More Reviews

  • Just ask happy customers directly - most will say yes
  • Send a follow-up email after a purchase or service
  • Create a simple handout or card with instructions
  • Use a QR code that goes straight to your review page
  • Make it as easy as humanly possible
  • Timing matters - ask soon after they've experienced your service

How to Handle Reviews the Right Way

  • Respond to everything: The good, the bad, everything
  • Be quick about it: Within 24-48 hours is ideal
  • Stay professional: Even when someone's being unfair
  • Make it personal: Use their name and reference specifics
  • Fix problems: When something's wrong, make it right
  • Say thank you: Appreciate the people who took time to review you

5. Optimizing Your Website for Local Search

Your actual website matters for local SEO too. Let's make sure it's sending the right signals.

Title Tags That Work Locally

  • Include your city or region in the title
  • Mention your main keyword
  • Add your business type if it makes sense
  • Keep it under 60 characters so it doesn't get cut off

Here's a good example:

Best Pizza Restaurant in Chicago | Giorno's Pizza

Meta Descriptions That Get Clicks

  • Mention your location and what you offer
  • Highlight what makes you different
  • Include a call to action
  • Stay under 160 characters

Put Your NAP Front and Center

  • Display it prominently on your contact page
  • Include it in your footer or sidebar
  • Use schema markup (more on that in a bit)
  • Make sure it matches your Google Business Profile exactly
  • Embed a Google Map while you're at it

Multiple Locations? Create Separate Pages

If you have more than one location, give each its own page:

  • Create a unique page for each location
  • Include that location's specific NAP info
  • Embed a map for each location
  • Add location-specific content and testimonials
  • Optimize for "near me" searches in that area
  • Don't just copy-paste the same content - make it unique

6. Creating Content That Ranks Locally

Content can help your local SEO too - when it's done right. Here's what works:

Content Ideas That Actually Work

  • Local guides: "Best parks in [city]" or "Things to do this weekend"
  • How-to content: "How to find a good plumber in [your city]"
  • Local news: Cover events happening in your area
  • Case studies: Show how you've helped local customers
  • Community stuff: Share your involvement locally

Blog Content That Builds Local Authority

  • Write about local events and activities
  • Highlight your community involvement
  • Showcase local projects and customers you've helped
  • Provide useful local resources and information
  • Address problems your local customers actually have

7. Building Links in Your Community

Links from local websites tell Google you're part of the community. Here's how to get them:

Where to Get Local Links

  • Local business groups: Chamber of Commerce, BBB, local business associations
  • Local media: Newspapers, TV stations, radio stations
  • Local blogs: City and neighborhood blogs
  • Sponsorships: Local events, sports teams, charities
  • Partnerships: Other businesses you can work with
  • Local directories: City-specific and industry directories

Ways to Earn Those Links

  • Sponsor local events and charities
  • Get involved in community activities
  • Offer scholarships or internships to local students
  • Host workshops or events in your area
  • Create useful local resources or tools
  • Participate in local government initiatives

8. Schema Markup for Local Businesses

Schema markup is code that helps Google understand your business information. It sounds technical, but it's worth it.

LocalBusiness Schema Example

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Giorno's Pizza",
  "image": "https://www.example.com/pizza.jpg",
  "telephone": "+1-555-123-4567",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street",
    "addressLocality": "Chicago",
    "addressRegion": "IL",
    "postalCode": "60601",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": 41.8781,
    "longitude": -87.6298
  },
  "url": "https://www.example.com",
  "openingHoursSpecification": {
    "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification",
    "dayOfWeek": ["Monday", "Tuesday"],
    "opens": "11:00",
    "closes": "22:00"
  },
  "priceRange": "$$"
}
</script>

9. Tracking What's Actually Working

How do you know if all this is paying off? Let's look at what to measure.

Metrics Worth Watching

  • Map Pack rankings: Are you showing up in the top 3?
  • Regular rankings: How's your position in normal results?
  • Profile completeness: Google gives you a score - check it
  • Call clicks: How many people call from your listing?
  • Direction requests: Are people asking for directions to you?
  • Website traffic: How many visitors from local search?

Tools to Track Your Progress

  • Google Business Profile insights: Built right into your profile
  • Google Search Console: See what's bringing people to your site
  • Google Analytics: Track traffic and conversions
  • Third-party tools: BrightLocal, Whitespark, Moz Local
