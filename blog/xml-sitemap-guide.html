Complete XML Sitemap Guide

Master sitemaps for better crawling and indexing

25 min read Updated Dec 30, 2024

Think of an XML sitemap like a roadmap you hand to Google. Sure, search engines can find their way around your site on their own, but why leave it to chance? A good sitemap makes sure your most important pages get discovered and indexed properly.

Let me walk you through everything you need to know about sitemaps—from the basics to some pro tips most people miss. Trust me, your site will thank you.

So, What's an XML Sitemap Anyway?

An XML sitemap is essentially a list of all the important pages on your website that you want search engines to know about. It's like saying, "Hey Google, here's everything that matters on my site." Pretty simple, right?

Crawl Discovery

Makes sure crawlers find all your important pages

Faster Indexing

New stuff shows up in search results faster

Extra Metadata

Gives Google helpful info like last update dates

Different Flavors of Sitemaps

Not all sitemaps are created equal. Depending on what kind of site you have, you might want to use different types:

XML Sitemap

The standard choice for most websites. Just lists your URLs with some optional info about each.

sitemap.xml

News Sitemap

If you run a news site, this one's for you. It includes publication dates and helps with Google News.

news-sitemap.xml

Video Sitemap

Got a lot of video content? This includes thumbnails, durations, and other video-specific info.

video-sitemap.xml

Image Sitemap

Image-heavy sites (like photography portfolios) can benefit from this specialized format.

image-sitemap.xml

Rolling Your Own Sitemap

You can create an XML sitemap manually if you want, but honestly? Most of the time it's easier to use a plugin or tool. Still, it's good to understand what's going on under the hood:

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<urlset xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9">

<url>
<loc>https://example.com/</loc>
<lastmod>2024-12-30</lastmod>
<changefreq>weekly</changefreq>
<priority>1.0</priority>
</url>

<url>
<loc>https://example.com/about</loc>
<lastmod>2024-12-28</lastmod>
<changefreq>monthly</changefreq>
<priority>0.8</priority>
</url>

</urlset>

Breaking Down the Tags

<loc>

Your page URL—this one's required

<lastmod>

When you last updated the page (optional but helpful)

<changefreq>

How often the page changes: always, hourly, daily, weekly, monthly, yearly, or never

<priority>

Relative importance from 0.0 to 1.0 (honesty helps here)

Sitemap Do's and Don'ts

After working with hundreds of sitemaps, I've learned what works and what doesn't. Here's the quick version:

Do's

  • Include all your important pages (canonical URLs only)
  • Keep sitemaps under 50MB and 50,000 URLs each
  • Split big sitemaps into multiple files with a sitemap index
  • Update your sitemap whenever you add new content
  • Put sitemap.xml right in your website's root folder

Don'ts

  • Skip redirects, canonicalized pages, and noindex pages
  • Leave out admin pages, login pages, and search results
  • Don't mix HTTP and HTTPS URLs in one sitemap
  • Avoid URLs with session IDs or weird parameters

When One Sitemap Isn't Enough

Got a big site with tons of pages? You can use a sitemap index file to organize multiple sitemaps. Think of it like a table of contents for your sitemaps:

<sitemapindex xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9">
<sitemap>
<loc>https://example.com/sitemap1.xml</loc>
</sitemap>
<sitemap>
<loc>https://example.com/sitemap2.xml</loc>
</sitemap>
</sitemapindex>

Getting Your Sitemap Out There

Creating the sitemap is step one. Now you need to make sure search engines actually know it exists. Here's how:

Google Search Console

  1. Add your property to GSC
  2. Go to "Sitemaps" section
  3. Enter your sitemap URL
  4. Click "Submit"
https://search.google.com/search-console

Bing Webmaster Tools

  1. Add your site to Bing Webmaster
  2. Go to "Sitemaps" section
  3. Submit your sitemap
https://www.bing.com/webmasters

One more thing: You can (and should) also reference your sitemap in robots.txt:

Sitemap: https://example.com/sitemap.xml
